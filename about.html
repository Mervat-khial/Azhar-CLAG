History of Al Azhar

  • 970 CE: Founded by the Fatimid Caliphate in Cairo, Egypt.
  • 11th Century: Transitioned from a Shia Ismaili center to a Sunni institution under the Abbasid Caliphate.
  • 12th Century: Gained prominence as a leading Sunni institution of higher learning.
  • Modern Era: Underwent modernization and reforms to adapt to contemporary challenges while preserving traditional Islamic teachings.
  • Present: Continues to be a renowned center for Islamic education and scholarship globally, influencing religious discourse and attracting students from around the world.

Al Azhar and the Palestinian cause

Al-Azhar, a renowned Islamic institution, stands in solidarity with the Palestinian cause. It advocates for the rights of Palestinians, condemns the occupation of their territories, and supports their quest for self-determination and a just resolution to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.

Al Azhar and the Arab World

Al-Azhar University stands as a beacon of pride for the Arab world, cherished for its rich history, esteemed scholars, and profound contributions to Islamic learning. Across the Arab world, countless students aspire to study within its venerable halls, drawn by its reputation for academic excellence and religious scholarship. Al-Azhar's teachings resonate deeply with Arab communities, reflecting cultural values and spiritual heritage. Its role as a center of Islamic knowledge and enlightenment continues to inspire generations, fostering a profound sense of reverence and admiration throughout the Arab world.
