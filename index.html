Al-Azhar University

The Al-Azhar University جامعة الأزهر (الشريف), is a public university in Cairo, Egypt. Associated with Al-Azhar Al-Sharif in Islamic Cairo, it is Egypt's oldest degree-granting university and is known as one of the most prestigious universities for Islamic learning.

Al-Azhar Mosque

Al-Azhar Mosque is in Cairo, Egypt in the historic Islamic core of the city.It was the first mosque established in a city that eventually earned the nickname "the City of a Thousand Minarets". Its name is usually thought to derive from az-Zahrāʾ, a title given to Fatimah, the daughter of Muhammad.
